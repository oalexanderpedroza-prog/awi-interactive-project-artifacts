00 / Research Question

The question that drove the work

ChatGPT is widely perceived as accessible because it uses natural language. But accessibility at the surface level is not the same as capability support. Effective use still requires skills that the interface rarely teaches — and the gap between who can access a tool and who can actually benefit from it is a design problem, not a user problem.

This research asks: how can ChatGPT improve early interaction success to increase activation, reduce disengagement, improve trust calibration, and support broader adoption?

My Stance
I approach this as a behavioral scientist, not a critic of technology. The question isn't whether AI tools are good or bad — it's whether the environments we build around them support or erode human capacity.

People aren't failing to use these tools because they lack intelligence. They're encountering ecological mismatches — between what the system demands and what their current conditions allow. That's a design problem. And design problems have design solutions.

Flourishing is a function of fit. My job is to make the fit better.
01 / Findings

Five interaction patterns
that shape engagement

01
Effective prompting relies on implicit skills the interface doesn't scaffold
Context specification, goal clarification, constraint articulation — these behaviors are rarely explained. Users unfamiliar with them assume the first response is the system's ceiling.
Supporting first-session success increases likelihood of sustained engagement
02
Cognitive load directly reduces interaction persistence
Users approaching ChatGPT under time pressure, information overload, or task complexity are less likely to experiment with prompt variations — not because they don't know how, but because they don't have bandwidth.
Reducing cognitive friction increases task completion and reduces abandonment
03
Trust formation depends on interpretability of system behavior
Users vary significantly in how they evaluate AI outputs — some use factual confidence signals, others use tone or fluency. When reliability signals are ambiguous, users either overtrust, undertrust, or restrict use to low-stakes tasks.
Improving transparency strengthens calibrated trust — and appropriate reliance
04
Mental model clarity predicts depth of engagement
Users who conceptualize ChatGPT as a collaborative, iterative tool show more adaptive interaction patterns. Users who see it as a one-shot answer engine underutilize capabilities and miss higher-value use cases.
Interface cues can cultivate more effective mental models through use
05
Early friction is the strongest predictor of disengagement
Pre-prompt uncertainty — how specific to be, what to include, whether the tool is appropriate — produces hesitation before users even begin. This front-loaded friction is rarely designed around, yet it determines whether first-session outcomes are strong enough to generate a second session.
Reducing pre-prompt ambiguity is the highest-leverage early activation intervention
01A / Research Synthesis

From observation to mechanism-level insight

Qualitative observation suggested that conversational AI friction often begins before interaction becomes substantive. Users were not simply struggling with output quality. Many were struggling to translate real-world problems into structured requests, calibrate trust in responses, and decide whether iteration was even part of the interaction model. These patterns informed the Human Outcomes Mapping Canvas used later in the case study.

Observed pattern
Task translation friction
Users often began with vague prompts, omitted context, and expected the system to infer situational details. Prompt formulation functioned as a hidden access barrier rather than a neutral first step.
Mechanism
Cognitive translation burden
Effective use required implicit capabilities: problem articulation, audience awareness, contextual specification, and tolerance for ambiguity under pressure.
Behavioral effect
Early hesitation and low iteration
Many participants treated ChatGPT as a one-turn answer engine. When first outputs were weak, interaction often ended before collaborative use patterns could emerge.
Design implication
Pre-prompt scaffolding is high leverage
Goal clarification, context prompts, interpretability cues, and iteration guidance can reduce early friction while broadening who reaches value quickly.
Theoretical Framework

The Wellness Verified
Evaluation Framework

Wellness Verified is a meta-framework for evaluating whether digital environments actively support — or systematically undermine — human functioning across behavioral, cognitive, emotional, and social domains.

The core thesis: wellness is the emergent outcome of the dynamic interplay between intrapersonal, interpersonal, and socio-technical-ecological systems across time. It is not a feature you add. It is a property of the fit between a person and their environment.

Applied to AI interaction design, this framework asks whether the system expands or constrains user capability — not just in the moment, but across conditions, contexts, and types of users.

Think of it as a LEED certification model for digital product design: a structured, multi-domain lens for asking whether an environment actually earns the word supportive.

Applied Criteria — ChatGPT

Does the interface reduce unnecessary cognitive burden on users?
Does the system support clarity of thinking rather than replacing it?
Does the interaction improve user confidence in completing real tasks?
Does the system help users achieve meaningful outcomes more efficiently?
Does the interface adapt to variability in user conditions and contexts?
Does the design support calibrated trust — neither overdependence nor avoidance?

"Capability-supportive environments don't just help users succeed. They expand who can succeed — and that is both an ethical obligation and a product growth strategy."

— Wellness Verified Framework · AWI Studio

02 / Core Artifact

Wellness Verified Human Outcomes
Mapping Canvas

To make the Wellness Verified lens operational inside a product workflow, I translated the framework into a structured synthesis artifact: the Human Outcomes Mapping Canvas. The tool is used after qualitative synthesis to trace how interface conditions influence behavioral mechanisms, how those mechanisms shape user capability, and how those shifts affect both human and product outcomes.

Rather than stopping at friction identification, the canvas makes causal logic more explicit. It helps distinguish whether a design issue is creating cognitive burden, weakening trust calibration, constraining perceived agency, or limiting the user's ability to form a collaborative mental model of the system.

04 / Design Opportunities

Five interventions that
reduce friction and expand access

🎯
Pre-Prompt Guidance
Support users in clarifying intent before interaction begins. Goal clarification prompts, audience identification, and optional structured input fields reduce first-prompt ambiguity.
↑ Activation success · ↑ First-response relevance
⚡
Input-Strength Feedback
Signal when additional context could improve output quality — through suggested questions or completeness cues. Teaches effective prompting through use, reducing reliance on external resources.
↓ Reliance on external prompt education · ↑ Skill formation
🔎
Trust Calibration Support
Improve interpretability through structured signals: confidence indicators, verification prompts for factual outputs, and cues for appropriate critical review. Reduces both overreliance and avoidance.
↓ Overtrust · ↓ Undertrust · ↑ Responsible use patterns
🔄
Iteration Normalization
Make multi-step interaction the visible default — not a workaround. Suggested follow-up prompts and visible conversational progression encourage higher-value use case discovery.
↑ Engagement depth · ↑ Feature discovery
🌐
Adaptive Interaction Support
Let guidance level adjust to demonstrated user behavior — more structure for first-time interactions, minimal interruption for experienced users. Improves usability across the full distribution without penalizing power users.
↑ Adoption breadth · Neutral impact on expert UX
🧭
The Unifying Principle
None of these are charity features for "struggling users." They are precision interventions that expand the percentage of users who reach meaningful value quickly. Value realization speed is the strongest predictor of habit formation.
Capability design = growth design

06 / Reflection

"AI systems are increasingly functioning as thinking environments. Which means the design decisions inside them are not neutral. They are decisions about who gets to think clearly, and who doesn't."

I conducted this research because I believe we are at a critical inflection point. The tools that will shape how people learn, solve problems, and connect with information are being designed right now — and most of them are being evaluated on engagement metrics that don't distinguish between productive use and compulsive use, or between trust and dependence.

The Wellness Verified framework exists because I needed a structured way to ask better questions. Not "is this tool being used?" but "does this tool expand what its users are capable of?" Not "is engagement high?" but "is the engagement building something, or eroding something?"

This case study is one application of that lens. But the lens is designed to be portable — applicable to any digital environment, any product, any platform that shapes human behavior at scale.

My goal as a researcher is not to be the person who points at what's broken. It's to be the person who expands what designers and product teams can see — and gives them conceptual tools to build better ecological fits between humans and the systems they rely on.
